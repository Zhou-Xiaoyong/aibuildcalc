Fence Installation Cost: Wood vs. Vinyl vs. Chain Link

Published July 2026 · 8 min read · By AIBuildCalc

Choosing a fence material involves balancing upfront cost, maintenance effort, privacy needs, and curb appeal. Wood offers classic looks at a moderate price. Vinyl promises low maintenance but costs more initially. Chain link is the most affordable but least private. This guide compares all three across the factors that matter most to homeowners.

Quick comparison: For a 150-linear-foot fence, expect roughly $2,000-$3,500 for chain link, $3,000-$5,500 for wood, and $4,500-$7,500 for vinyl. Use our Fence Calculator to estimate your exact project cost.

Upfront installation cost

Prices below are for a standard 6-foot tall residential fence installed by a professional contractor. Costs include posts, concrete, fasteners, and labor.

MaterialCost per Linear Foot150 ft Total
Chain Link$15-$25$2,250-$3,750
Wood (Pressure-Treated)$20-$35$3,000-$5,250
Wood (Cedar)$25-$45$3,750-$6,750
Vinyl (PVC)$30-$50$4,500-$7,500

Gate costs are additional. A standard 4-foot walk gate adds $150-$400 depending on material. A 10-foot double drive gate can add $500-$1,200.

Maintenance and lifespan

The true cost of a fence includes maintenance over its lifetime. Here is how the materials compare:

MaterialAnnual MaintenanceExpected Lifespan10-Year Cost*
Chain LinkMinimal — rust spot repair20-30 years$2,500-$4,000
Wood (Pressure-Treated)Stain/seal every 2-3 years15-20 years$4,500-$7,500
Wood (Cedar)Stain/seal every 2-3 years15-25 years$5,500-$9,000
VinylOccasional washing25-40 years$4,800-$7,800

*10-year cost includes initial installation plus estimated maintenance.

Privacy and aesthetics

  • Wood: Excellent privacy when built with no gaps between boards. Can be stained any color. Natural look that many homeowners prefer.
  • Vinyl: Solid privacy panels available in white, tan, and gray. Clean, modern appearance. Cannot be painted a different color later.
  • Chain link: No privacy without adding slats or planting hedges. Best for security and boundary marking, not privacy.

Which fence is right for you?

Choose chain link if you need an affordable boundary or pet enclosure and privacy is not a priority. Choose wood if you want traditional looks, privacy, and are willing to perform regular maintenance. Choose vinyl if you want a clean, modern appearance with minimal upkeep and can afford the higher upfront cost.

Ways to save on fence installation

  • Get quotes from at least 3 licensed, insured fence contractors
  • Install in winter or early spring when demand is lower
  • Remove old fencing yourself to save on demolition costs
  • Use wood posts with wood panels instead of metal posts for wood fences
  • Opt for a shorter fence (4 ft vs. 6 ft) where code allows
