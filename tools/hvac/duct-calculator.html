How to Use This Calculator

Correctly sized ductwork ensures balanced airflow, quiet operation, and efficient HVAC performance. Undersized ducts restrict flow and strain equipment, while oversized ducts waste material and money.

  1. Determine the CFM required for the room or branch
  2. Set your target air velocity (residential: 600 fpm)
  3. Enter both values to calculate the duct diameter
  4. Use the recommended shape to guide material selection

Velocity Optimization
Higher velocity shrinks duct size but increases noise and friction. AI balances 400-900 fpm for residential comfort and quiet operation.

Shape Selection Logic
Round ducts under 8 inches are quieter and more efficient. Larger runs benefit from rectangular profiles to save headroom.
📊
Friction Awareness
Every elbow and transition adds friction loss. AI encourages upsizing ducts slightly on long or complex runs to maintain airflow.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you calculate duct size from CFM?
Duct area (sq ft) = CFM / velocity (fpm). Convert to square inches (x144), then solve for diameter using the circle area formula. Standard residential velocity is 600 fpm.
What is the standard velocity for residential ducts?
Residential supply ducts typically run 600-900 fpm, while return ducts run 400-700 fpm. Lower velocities reduce noise but require larger ducts.
Round or rectangular ducts — which is better?
Round ducts are more efficient and quieter for small diameters (under 8 inches). Rectangular ducts save headroom in tight spaces and are used for larger runs.
