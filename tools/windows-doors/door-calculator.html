How to Use This Calculator

Framing a door opening? Getting the rough opening right is critical so the door fits, swings freely, and latches. Our calculator sizes the rough opening from your door slab dimensions.

  1. Measure the door slab width and height
  2. Enter the jamb thickness
  3. Run the calculator
  4. Frame the rough opening and allow for shimming and a level threshold

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you calculate rough opening for a door?
Add 2 inches to the door width (1 inch per side for the frame and shims) and 2.5 inches to the height (2 inches for the header plus 1.5 inches for the threshold and sill).
What are standard interior door sizes?
Standard interior doors are 28, 30, 32, and 36 inches wide by 80 inches tall. A 30x80 inch door is the most common for bedrooms and bathrooms.
What is a door jamb thickness?
Standard interior door jamb thickness is 4-9/16 inches for 2x4 walls and 6-9/16 inches for 2x6 walls. Exterior doors often use 1-3/4 inch thick slabs.
