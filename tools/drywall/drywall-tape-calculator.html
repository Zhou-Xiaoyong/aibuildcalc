How to Use This Calculator

Taping drywall seams? Accurate tape estimates prevent mid-project supply runs and reduce waste. Our calculator sizes tape by drywall area and seam density.

  1. Add up the total square footage of drywall to be taped
  2. Choose Standard for full-sheet installs or High for many small pieces
  3. Enter your values in the calculator
  4. Buy one extra roll for corners, repairs, and touch-ups

Frequently Asked Questions

How much drywall tape do I need per square foot?
For standard seam density, plan on about 1.3 feet of tape per square foot of drywall. High seam density (more joints, smaller sheets) raises this to about 1.6 feet per square foot.
How many feet of tape are on a roll?
A standard roll of drywall joint tape contains 250 feet. A box typically holds 24 rolls, or 6,000 feet total.
Should I use paper or fiberglass mesh drywall tape?
Paper tape is stronger and best for flat seams and corners with all-purpose mud. Fiberglass mesh tape is self-adhesive and easier for flat seams but should be used with setting-type compound.
