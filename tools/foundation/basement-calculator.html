How to Use This Calculator

Building a basement is one of the biggest line items in a new home. This tool estimates how much dirt comes out, how much concrete goes in, and what it costs to finish the space.

  1. Enter the length and width of the basement footprint
  2. Set the excavation depth (typical 8 ft for full basements)
  3. Choose Unfinished or Finished to update the cost per square foot
  4. Review excavation and concrete volumes plus total cost
Expert Insights Smart analysis for your Foundation project
🕳️
Excavation Volume
Excavation is footprint times depth divided by 27. Rocky or clay soil and high water tables can raise digging costs by 50% or more.
🏗️
Concrete Wall Estimate
Our model uses 8-inch poured walls at 8 ft tall — the standard basement wall. Thicker walls or deeper pours add concrete and forming cost.
🎨
Finish Cost per Sq Ft
Unfinished shells run about $25/sq ft; fully finished living space averages $55/sq ft including framing, drywall, electrical, and flooring.

Calculation Formula

Excavation (cu yd) = House length x House width x Depth / 27 Concrete (cu yd) = 2 x (House length + House width) x 8 x (8 / 12) / 27 Cost per sq ft = Finished ? $55 : $25 Estimated cost = ceil(House length x House width x Cost per sq ft)

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to dig a basement?
Excavation for a basement typically runs $20-30 per square foot unfinished and $50-70 finished. A 40x30 ft unfinished basement costs about $30,000, while a finished one runs around $66,000.
How much concrete is in a basement?
Basement walls use roughly 2 x (length + width) x wall height x (thickness / 12) / 27 cubic yards of concrete. A 40x30 ft basement with 8 ft, 8-inch walls needs about 12-13 cubic yards.
Is a finished basement worth it?
Finished basements add livable square footage at about $55 per sq ft — roughly half the cost of a home addition. They typically return 70-75% of cost at resale.
