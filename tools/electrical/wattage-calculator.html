How to Use This Calculator

Accurate wattage estimates help you size circuits, generators, and solar systems correctly. Enter your voltage, current, and power factor for instant results.

  1. Enter the circuit voltage (typically 120V or 240V)
  2. Enter the current draw in amps
  3. Set the power factor (1.0 for heaters, 0.85-0.9 for motors)
  4. Review watts, kilowatts, and equivalent amps at 240V

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you calculate watts from volts and amps?
For DC and resistive AC loads, watts = volts x amps. For AC loads with a power factor, multiply by the power factor: watts = volts x amps x power factor.
What is a power factor?
Power factor is the ratio of real power used by a load to apparent power delivered, ranging from 0 to 1. Resistive loads like heaters are 1.0, while motors may be 0.8-0.9.
How many amps is 1500 watts at 120V?
1500 watts divided by 120 volts equals 12.5 amps, which is why most space heaters use a dedicated 15-amp circuit.
