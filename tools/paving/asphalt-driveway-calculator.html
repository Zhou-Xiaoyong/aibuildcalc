How to Use This Calculator

Asphalt driveway costs depend on area, thickness, base preparation, and local material prices. Our calculator helps you estimate material quantities and compare against contractor quotes.

  1. Measure driveway length and width in feet
  2. Select asphalt thickness (2-3" residential, 4" heavy duty)
  3. Enter your local price per ton (includes delivery and installation)
  4. Add base gravel depth if installing new (not overlay)
  5. Get total cost estimate for budgeting
Expert Insights Smart analysis for your paving project
🛣️
Overlay vs. Remove & Replace
An overlay (1.5-2" over existing asphalt) costs 40-50% less than full removal. However, if the base is failing or cracks are severe, removal is necessary.
🌡️
Climate Considerations
Cold climates need thicker bases (6-8") and asphalt with polymer modifiers. Hot climates require stiffer asphalt grades to prevent rutting.
📅
Seasonal Pricing
Asphalt plants close in winter in northern climates. Scheduling in late spring or early fall can save 10-15% compared to peak summer demand.

Calculation Formula

Area (sq ft) = Length x Width Asphalt Volume (cu ft) = Area x (Thickness / 12) Asphalt Volume (cu yd) = Volume (cu ft) / 27 Tons of Asphalt = Volume (cu ft) x 110 lbs/cu ft / 2000 Base Volume (cu yd) = Area x (Base Depth / 12) / 27

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does an asphalt driveway cost?
Asphalt driveway costs average $3-$5 per square foot for a standard 2-inch overlay, or $5-$8 per square foot for full-depth removal and replacement. A typical 2-car driveway (20x24 ft) costs $1,500-$4,000.
How thick should an asphalt driveway be?
Residential driveways should be 2-3 inches of asphalt compacted over 4-6 inches of crushed stone base. Heavy vehicles or RVs require 4 inches of asphalt.
How many tons of asphalt per square foot?
At 2 inches thick, asphalt weighs approximately 0.055 tons per square foot (110 lbs/sq ft). At 3 inches, use 0.0825 tons/sq ft.
