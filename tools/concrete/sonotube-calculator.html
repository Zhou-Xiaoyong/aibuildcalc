How to Use This Calculator

Sonotubes are cardboard forms used for deck and porch footings. Our calculator estimates concrete volume and cost per tube.

  1. Determine frost line depth in your area
  2. Choose sonotube diameter based on deck load
  3. Enter tube height and quantity
  4. Add 10% extra for form variances

Slab Thickness Guide
4" for patios, 5-6" for driveways, 6-8" for heavy vehicle loads. Our calculator references your dimensions against ACI 301-20 standards for optimal thickness recommendations.
📊
Bag vs Ready-Mix Analysis
For pours under 1 cubic yard, 80lb bags cost less. Over 3 yards, ready-mix saves 30-50%. Our calculator factors your volume against regional pricing.
🛡️
Curing & Strength Timeline
Concrete reaches 50% strength in 3 days, 70% in 7 days, and full cure in 28 days. Cold weather? Our calculator flags when heated enclosures are needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size sonotube do I need for a deck?
8-12" sonotubes are standard for residential decks. For a 12x12 deck with 6 footings, use 12"x48" sonotubes.
How much concrete per sonotube?
A 12"x48" sonotube holds about 3.14 cubic feet or about 5 bags of 80lb concrete. Plan for 6 bags per tube for safety.
