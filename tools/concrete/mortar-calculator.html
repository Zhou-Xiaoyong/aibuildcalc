How to Use This Calculator

Mixing the right amount of mortar keeps your masonry project moving without waste. Our calculator estimates mortar bags, sand, and water based on wall area and joint thickness.

  1. Enter the total wall area in square feet
  2. Set the mortar joint thickness (3/8" is standard)
  3. Choose your brick size for accurate yield
  4. Review the mortar bags, sand, and water estimates

Bag Yield Intelligence
An 80 lb bag of premixed mortar yields about 0.7 cubic feet. Our calculator converts your joint volume into accurate bag counts.

Mortar Type Selection
Type N suits above-grade walls; Type S handles load-bearing and below-grade. Always match mortar type to the structural requirement.
💧
Mix Consistency
Too much water weakens mortar. Add roughly 1.25 gallons per 80 lb bag and adjust slowly until the mix holds a slump similar to peanut butter.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much mortar do I need per square foot?
For a standard 3/8" mortar joint, expect about 0.044 cubic feet of mortar per square foot of wall, or roughly one 80 lb bag per 22-23 sq ft of wall area.
What is the difference between Type N and Type S mortar?
Type N is a general-purpose mortar for above-grade walls with moderate strength (750 psi). Type S has higher strength (1,800 psi) and is used for load-bearing and below-grade applications.
How much water do I add to a bag of mortar?
An 80 lb bag of premixed mortar requires about 1.25 gallons (5 quarts) of clean water. Add water gradually until the mix reaches a workable, buttery consistency.
