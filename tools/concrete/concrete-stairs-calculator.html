How to Use This Calculator

Building concrete stairs requires precise volume calculations. Our calculator sums up all step volumes to give you an accurate estimate.

  1. Determine number of steps needed
  2. Set riser height (7" standard) and tread depth (11" standard)
  3. Enter the stair width
  4. Add 10% extra for form irregularities

Slab Thickness Guide
4" for patios, 5-6" for driveways, 6-8" for heavy vehicle loads. Our calculator references your dimensions against ACI 301-20 standards for optimal thickness recommendations.

Bag vs Ready-Mix Analysis
For pours under 1 cubic yard, 80lb bags cost less. Over 3 yards, ready-mix saves 30-50%. Our calculator factors your volume against regional pricing.
🛡️
Curing & Strength Timeline
Concrete reaches 50% strength in 3 days, 70% in 7 days, and full cure in 28 days. Cold weather? Our calculator flags when heated enclosures are needed.

Calculation Formula

Volume per step = Width x Tread x Riser Total = Sum of all step volumes / 27

Frequently Asked Questions

What are standard step dimensions?
Standard residential steps: 7" riser and 11" tread (7-11 rule). Max riser height is 7.75" per IRC code.
How much concrete for 4 steps?
Four steps (4ft wide, 7" riser, 11" tread) need approximately 0.5 cubic yards or about 23 bags of 80lb concrete.
