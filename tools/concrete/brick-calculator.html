How to Use This Calculator

Building a brick wall? Accurate material estimates keep your project on budget and reduce waste. Our calculator accounts for brick dimensions and mortar joint thickness for a precise count.

  1. Measure the total length and height of your wall in feet
  2. Confirm your brick dimensions (standard modular is 8" x 2.25")
  3. Enter the mortar joint thickness (3/8" is standard)
  4. The calculator adds a 5% waste factor for cuts and breakage
Expert Insights Smart analysis for your Brick project
🧱
Brick Type Intelligence
Modular, queen, and king bricks have different face dimensions. Our calculator adjusts the per-square-foot count based on your exact brick size and joint.
🪣
Mortar Yield Optimization
One 80 lb bag of premixed mortar lays roughly 30 standard bricks. Field conditions and joint depth can shift yield by 10-15%.
📏
Waste Factor Guidance
Straight walls need ~5% waste. Walls with many openings, corners, or soldier courses typically require 10-15% extra bricks.

Calculation Formula

Effective width = Brick length + Mortar joint (in) Effective height = Brick height + Mortar joint (in) Bricks per sq ft = 144 / (Effective width x Effective height) Wall area = Wall length x Wall height (sq ft) Bricks = ceil(Wall area x Bricks per sq ft x 1.05) (5% waste) Mortar bags = ceil(Bricks / 30) Sand (cu yd) = ceil(Bricks / 500)

Frequently Asked Questions

How many bricks do I need per square foot?
A standard modular brick (8" x 2.25" with a 3/8" mortar joint) covers about 7 bricks per square foot of wall area.
How much mortar do I need for 1000 bricks?
Approximately 33 bags of 80 lb premixed mortar are required to lay 1000 standard bricks, plus about 1 cubic yard of masonry sand.
What waste factor should I add for bricks?
A 5% waste factor is standard for straight walls. Use 10-15% for walls with many cuts, corners, or openings.
