How to Use This Calculator

Proper footings are the foundation of any solid structure. Our concrete footing calculator helps you determine exactly how much concrete you need.

  1. Check local building codes for frost depth requirements
  2. Determine footing diameter based on structure load
  3. Enter footing dimensions and quantity
  4. Add 10% extra for variations in hole size
  5. Consider using tube forms

Slab Thickness Guide
4" for patios, 5-6" for driveways, 6-8" for heavy vehicle loads. Our calculator references your dimensions against ACI 301-20 standards for optimal thickness recommendations.
📊
Bag vs Ready-Mix Analysis
For pours under 1 cubic yard, 80lb bags cost less. Over 3 yards, ready-mix saves 30-50%. Our calculator factors your volume against regional pricing.
🛡️
Curing & Strength Timeline
Concrete reaches 50% strength in 3 days, 70% in 7 days, and full cure in 28 days. Cold weather? Our calculator flags when heated enclosures are needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

How deep should concrete footings be?
Footings should extend below the frost line (12"-60"+ depending on state). Check local building codes.
What diameter should deck footings be?
Standard deck footings are 12"-16" in diameter for residential decks. Larger decks may need 18"-24".
How much concrete per footing?
A 12" diameter footing that is 36" deep needs about 0.24 cubic feet or roughly 1 bag of 80lb concrete.
