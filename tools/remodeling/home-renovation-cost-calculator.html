How to Use This Calculator

Home renovation costs vary dramatically by room type, finish quality, and location. This calculator uses 2026 national cost data adjusted by region to help you budget realistically.

  1. Select the room or area you plan to renovate
  2. Enter the square footage (measure wall to wall)
  3. Choose a finish level that matches your budget and goals
  4. Select your regional cost multiplier
  5. Decide if you need demolition of existing finishes
Expert Insights Smart analysis for your renovation budget
💰
The 30% Rule
Never spend more than 30% of your home's current value on a single renovation. This protects your investment and prevents over-improving for the neighborhood.
📊
Hidden Cost Buffer
Add 15-20% contingency for unforeseen issues (water damage, outdated wiring, asbestos). Older homes (pre-1980) may need 25% contingency.
🏠
ROI by Project
Kitchens and bathrooms typically return 50-75% of cost. Exterior improvements (siding, roofing) return 60-80%. Swimming pools often return less than 50%.

Calculation Methodology

Base Cost = Area x Cost per Sq Ft (by room type and finish level) Location Adjustment = Base Cost x Location Factor Demolition = Area x $3-$8/sq ft (if selected) Labor = Adjusted Cost x 0.55 Materials = Adjusted Cost x 0.45

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a home renovation cost per square foot?
Home renovation costs typically range from $25-$75 per square foot for cosmetic updates, $75-$150 for mid-grade remodels, and $150-$300+ for high-end renovations. Kitchens and bathrooms cost more per square foot than living areas.
What is the most expensive room to renovate?
Kitchens are the most expensive room to renovate, averaging $150-$300 per square foot due to cabinets, countertops, appliances, and plumbing. Bathrooms are second, averaging $125-$250 per square foot.
Should I remodel or move?
If you like your location and the renovation cost is less than 30% of your home's value, remodeling is usually the better financial decision. Moving involves 8-10% in transaction costs alone.
