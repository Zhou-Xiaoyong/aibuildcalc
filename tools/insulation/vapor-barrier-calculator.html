How to Use This Calculator

Vapor barriers need overlap at the seams and a waste allowance to cover the full floor. This tool sizes your poly rolls and seam tape in one pass.

  1. Measure the floor or crawlspace area in square feet
  2. Enter the seam overlap (6 inches is standard)
  3. Set the roll width and length from the product label
  4. Seal every seam and penetration with vapor-barrier tape

Frequently Asked Questions

What mil thickness should a vapor barrier be?
Use 6 mil polyethylene as a minimum for crawlspaces and floors. For high-moisture areas or long-term durability, 10 to 15 mil reinforced poly is recommended.
How much should vapor barrier sheets overlap?
Overlap seams by at least 6 inches and seal them with vapor-barrier tape. At penetrations and edges, wrap the poly up the wall 6-12 inches and seal to the foundation.
Where should a vapor barrier be installed?
In cold climates, install vapor barriers on the warm (interior) side of walls and ceilings. In hot, humid climates, exterior vapor retarders are often preferred to avoid trapping moisture.
