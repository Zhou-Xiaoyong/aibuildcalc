How to Use This Calculator

Total R-value determines how well your assembly resists heat flow. Our calculator adds up thermal resistance of each layer.

  1. Identify each material layer
  2. Enter R-value for each layer
  3. Calculator sums all R-values
  4. Compare against climate zone code

R-Value by Climate Zone
Attic: R-30 to R-60 depending on zone. Walls: R-13 to R-20+. AI maps your region to DOE climate zone recommendations.
💨
Air Sealing Impact
Air sealing before insulating improves efficiency 20-40%. AI calculates the combined R-value benefit of sealing + insulating.
📊
Batt vs Blown-In vs Spray Foam
Batts: lowest cost, DIY-friendly. Blown-in: better coverage. Spray foam: air seals + insulates. AI compares cost per R-value for each.

Calculation Formula

Total R = R1 + R2 + R3 + ... (sum of all layers) U-Factor = 1 / Total R

Frequently Asked Questions

What R-value is required by code?
IRC 2021: Walls R-20 or R-13+5ci, Ceilings R-49, Floors R-19, Basements R-15 continuous or R-19 cavity.
What is a U-factor?
U-factor measures heat transfer rate - lower is better. U = 1/R. Codes specify maximum U-factors.
