How to Use This Calculator

A well-sized vanity balances storage, sink space, and walkway clearance. Accurate dimensions help you order the right cabinet, countertop, and plumbing fixtures.

  1. Measure the available wall width for the vanity
  2. Choose single or double sink layout
  3. Calculate to get vanity width and countertop area
  4. Use the cost estimate to budget your remodel

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the standard bathroom vanity width?
Single-sink vanities are typically 24-48 inches wide, while double-sink vanities range from 60-72 inches. Width should match the available wall space with 1-2 inches of clearance.
How much countertop do I need for a vanity?
Countertop area is roughly 1.5 times the wall width in square feet, allowing for a standard 22-inch depth plus an overhang. Double-sink vanities need more surface area.
How much does a bathroom vanity cost?
Vanities cost about $200 per linear foot installed. Single-sink units run $300-1,500, while double-sink vanities range $800-3,000 depending on material and countertop type.
