How to Use This Calculator

Ordering the right amount of shower tile prevents costly shortages and excess waste. This calculator covers all three walls plus the floor of a standard shower enclosure.

  1. Measure the shower width and depth
  2. Enter the wall height of the shower
  3. Calculate to get the total tile area and box count
  4. Use the cost estimate to plan your tile budget
Expert Insights Smart analysis for your Kitchen & Bath project

Full Surface Coverage
AI sums three walls (perimeter x height) plus the floor (width x depth) so you order tile for every shower surface.

Waste-Aware Box Count
Tile is sold in 10 sq ft boxes. AI adds 10% for cuts and breakage before dividing, so you do not run short mid-install.

Budget Benchmarking
Installed shower tile averages $12 per sq ft. AI multiplies area by the benchmark so you can spot overpriced quotes.

Calculation Formula

Tile Area = 2 x (Width + Depth) x Height + (Width x Depth) Boxes = ceil(Area x 1.1 / 10) (10 sq ft per box, 10% waste) Estimated Cost = ceil(Area x $12)

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate tile for a shower?
Measure each wall (width x height) and the floor (width x depth), then add them together. Include 10% extra for cuts and waste. Divide total sq ft by the sq ft per box to find box count.
How much tile do I need for a standard shower?
A typical 4x4 ft shower with 8 ft walls needs about 80-90 sq ft of tile, including the floor. Add 10% waste for a total of roughly 90-100 sq ft.
How much does shower tile cost per square foot?
Ceramic shower tile runs $2-15 per sq ft, porcelain $3-20, and natural stone $7-30. Installation adds $8-15 per sq ft, bringing the average installed cost to about $12 per sq ft.
