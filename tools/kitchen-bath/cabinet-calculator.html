How to Use This Calculator

Accurate cabinet estimates keep your kitchen remodel on budget. Knowing your linear feet and cabinet count helps you compare stock, semi-custom, and custom options.

  1. Measure the wall length available for cabinets
  2. Choose the cabinet type (base, wall, or tall)
  3. Calculate to get linear feet and cabinet count

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do kitchen cabinets cost per linear foot?
Stock base cabinets run about $250 per linear foot, wall cabinets $200, and tall cabinets $300. Custom cabinetry can reach $500-1,200 per linear foot.
How many cabinets do I need for my kitchen?
Most cabinets come in 3-foot sections. Divide your total wall length by 3 to estimate the cabinet count. A typical 10x10 kitchen uses 12-15 cabinets.
What is the difference between base, wall, and tall cabinets?
Base cabinets sit on the floor (34.5 in high), wall cabinets mount above counters (12-42 in high), and tall cabinets run floor-to-ceiling (84-96 in) for pantries and utilities.
