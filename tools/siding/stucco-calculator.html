How to Use This Calculator

Stucco material needs scale with the number of coats. A traditional 3-coat system uses far more cement than a 1-coat EIFS assembly.

  1. Measure the total wall area to be covered
  2. Choose the stucco system: 3-coat traditional or 1-coat EIFS
  3. Enter dimensions in the calculator
  4. Order extra bags for corner beads, control joints, and touch-ups

Coat System Tuning
A 3-coat system needs 3x the cement of a 1-coat EIFS job. AI multiplies bag and sand quantities by the correct coat count.
🏖️
Sand Proportioning
Stucco mix uses roughly 1 cubic yard of sand per 324 sq ft per coat. AI sizes sand delivery to match your batch plan.
💧
Cure & Weather
Stucco needs 48 hours of curing between the scratch and brown coats. Avoid application below 40°F or above 90°F to prevent cracking.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many bags of stucco do I need per square foot?
A 3-coat stucco system needs about 1 bag (80 lb) per 48 sq ft of wall, while a 1-coat EIFS system needs roughly 1 bag per 144 sq ft because it applies a thinner base coat.
What is the difference between 3-coat stucco and EIFS?
Traditional 3-coat stucco uses a scratch, brown, and finish coat of cement over wire lath. EIFS (Exterior Insulation and Finish System) is a 1-coat synthetic system over rigid foam insulation that is lighter and more energy efficient.
How much does stucco cost per square foot?
Stucco installation averages $6-$12 per square foot for materials and labor. A typical 1,000 sq ft wall costs $6,000-$12,000 depending on system type and finish.
