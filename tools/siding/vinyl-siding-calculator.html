How to Use This Calculator

Vinyl siding is sold by the square (100 sq ft). Subtracting openings and adding a waste factor keeps your order tight and your budget realistic.

  1. Measure the total exterior wall area
  2. Subtract door and window openings
  3. Add a waste percentage for cuts and breaks
  4. Order extra accessories: J-channel, starter strip, and corner posts
Expert Insights Smart analysis for your Siding project
📏
Opening Deduction
Subtracting windows and doors from gross wall area avoids buying 1-2 extra squares of siding you will never install.
✂️
Waste Factor Tuning
Simple rectangular walls need ~10% waste, while complex homes with dormers and many corners push it to 15-20%. AI scales to your layout.
💲
Cost Benchmarks
Installed vinyl siding runs $250 per square on average. Premium insulated panels and custom colors can reach $400+ per square.

Calculation Formula

Net Area = Wall Area - Openings Squares = ceil(Net Area x (1 + Waste% / 100) / 100) Linear Feet = Squares x 100 Cost = ceil(Squares x $250)

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a square of vinyl siding?
A square of vinyl siding equals 100 square feet of coverage. Siding is sold by the square, so a 1,000 sq ft wall needs about 10 squares plus waste.
How much waste should I add for vinyl siding?
Plan on 10% waste for simple gable walls, 15% for homes with many windows and corners, and up to 20% for complex layouts with dormers or multiple wall heights.
How much does vinyl siding cost per square?
Vinyl siding averages $100-$250 per square for materials and $250-$400 per square installed, including accessories like J-channel, starter strip, and corner posts.
