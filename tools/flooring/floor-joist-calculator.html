How to Use This Calculator

Building a floor frame? Accurate joist counts prevent under-ordering lumber and keep your floor structurally sound. Our calculator counts joists based on spacing and computes total linear feet.

  1. Measure the width of the room the joists will span across
  2. Confirm your on-center joist spacing (16 in is standard residential)
  3. Enter the span (length) of each joist
  4. Order 1 extra joist as a spare for cuts and defects

Frequently Asked Questions

What is standard floor joist spacing?
Standard residential floor joist spacing is 16 inches on center. Spacing of 12 inches is used for heavier loads, and 24 inches for lighter loads and shorter spans.
How far can a 2x10 floor joist span?
A 2x10 floor joist at 16 inches on center can typically span 13-16 feet depending on wood species and grade. Always check span tables for your specific lumber.
What size are floor joists in residential construction?
Common floor joist sizes are 2x8, 2x10, and 2x12 nominal lumber, or engineered I-joists. The size depends on span length, spacing, and expected live load.
