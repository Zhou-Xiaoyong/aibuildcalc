How to Use This Calculator

Installing a subfloor? Accurate plywood estimates prevent material shortages and reduce waste. Our calculator sizes sheets for standard 4x8 panels with a 10% waste allowance.

  1. Measure the length and width of your room in feet
  2. Confirm your plywood sheet size (4x8 ft is standard)
  3. Enter your dimensions in the calculator
  4. Stagger panel seams and glue edges for a squeak-free floor
Expert Insights Smart analysis for your Flooring project
🪵
Panel Layout Optimization
Staggering plywood seams by half a panel strengthens the floor and reduces squeaks. AI factors 10% waste for cuts around walls and openings.
🔩
Fastener & Adhesive Strategy
Subfloor adhesive plus ring-shank screws every 6 in at edges and 12 in in the field prevents nail pops and floor movement.
📏
Thickness Selection
3/4 in T&G plywood for 16 in joist spacing. For 24 in spacing, use 7/8 in or engineered panels. AI matches thickness to your joist layout.

Frequently Asked Questions

What thickness plywood is used for subfloor?
Standard subfloor plywood is 5/8 inch or 3/4 inch thick T&G (tongue and groove) panels. Most residential construction uses 3/4 inch for joists spaced 16 inches on center.
How many square feet does a 4x8 sheet of plywood cover?
A standard 4x8 foot sheet of plywood covers 32 square feet. Always add 10% for waste due to cuts around walls and openings.
How much does subfloor plywood cost?
Subfloor-grade plywood (3/4 in T&G) averages $35-$50 per 4x8 sheet. OSB subfloor panels run slightly less at $25-$40 per sheet.
