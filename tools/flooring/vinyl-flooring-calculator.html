How to Use This Calculator

Planning a vinyl plank flooring project? Accurate material estimates prevent costly over-ordering and mid-project shortages. Our calculator accounts for waste and plank dimensions to give you a true material count.

  1. Measure the length and width of your room in feet
  2. Check the plank width printed on the carton (commonly 7 inches)
  3. Enter your dimensions in the calculator
  4. Keep the 10% waste factor for straight lays; raise it to 15% for diagonal layouts

Frequently Asked Questions

How many vinyl planks are in a carton?
Most vinyl plank cartons contain 12 planks and cover roughly 18-24 square feet, depending on plank dimensions.
How much waste should I add for vinyl flooring?
A 10% waste factor is standard for straight-lay vinyl plank installations. Use 15% for diagonal layouts or rooms with many corners.
How much does vinyl flooring cost per square foot?
Vinyl plank flooring averages $2-$5 per square foot for materials. Installation adds $2-$5 per square foot, bringing total cost to $4-$10 per square foot.
