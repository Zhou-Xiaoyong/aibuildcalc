How to Use This Calculator

Planning a linoleum flooring project? Linoleum is sold by the square yard in rolls, so accurate estimates prevent over-ordering and minimize seam waste. Our calculator accounts for roll width and waste.

  1. Measure the length and width of your room in feet
  2. Confirm the roll width available at your supplier (commonly 12 ft)
  3. Enter your dimensions in the calculator
  4. Keep the 10% waste factor for simple rooms; use 15% for irregular shapes

Frequently Asked Questions

How is linoleum sold?
Linoleum is sold by the square yard in rolls, commonly 6 or 12 feet wide. One square yard equals 9 square feet.
What is the standard roll width for linoleum?
The most common residential linoleum roll width is 12 feet, which covers most rooms in a single sheet with minimal seams.
How much does linoleum flooring cost?
Linoleum averages $3-$5 per square yard for materials. Professional installation adds $2-$4 per square yard, bringing total cost to $5-$9 per square yard.
