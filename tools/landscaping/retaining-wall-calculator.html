How to Use This Calculator

Building a block retaining wall? Accurate block counts prevent shortages and extra trips to the supply yard. Our calculator figures blocks, cap blocks, and cost from your wall and block dimensions.

  1. Measure the total length and height of your wall
  2. Check your block dimensions (most standard blocks are 8" x 16")
  3. Enter the wall and block sizes into the calculator
  4. Add 5-10% extra blocks for cuts, breakage, and waste

Block Geometry Smarts
Our calculator converts wall feet to inches and rounds up each row and column so partial blocks count as full units — preventing mid-project shortages.
🏗️
Height Code Check
Walls over 4 feet usually require engineered design and a permit. If your wall approaches 4 feet, plan for a building permit and possibly a geotechnical review.
💧
Drainage Reminder
Every retaining wall needs gravel backfill and a drainage pipe behind the base. Hydrostatic pressure is the #1 cause of retaining wall failure.

Calculation Formula

Blocks per row = Ceiling(Wall length × 12 / Block length) Rows = Ceiling(Wall height × 12 / Block height) Total blocks = Blocks per row × Rows Cap blocks = Blocks per row Estimated cost = Total blocks × $3.00/block

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate how many blocks I need for a retaining wall?
Divide the wall length in inches by the block length to get blocks per row, then divide the wall height in inches by the block height to get rows. Multiply the two numbers together for total blocks.
How tall can a retaining wall be without a permit?
Most US cities allow retaining walls up to 3-4 feet tall without an engineered design or permit. Walls over 4 feet typically require a permit, footing design, and possibly a geotechnical engineer.
Do I need a cap on a retaining wall?
Yes. Cap blocks protect the top course from water intrusion and give the wall a finished look. Use one cap block per block in the top row, secured with construction adhesive.
