How to Use This Calculator

Building a block retaining wall? Accurate block counts prevent costly supply runs and keep your project on schedule. Our calculator accounts for wall length, height, and your specific block dimensions.

  1. Measure the total length and exposed height of your wall
  2. Check the dimensions printed on your block packaging
  3. Enter wall and block dimensions into the calculator
  4. Add 5-10% extra blocks for cuts, breaks, and waste
Expert Insights Smart analysis for your Retaining Wall project
🧱
Block Style Smarts
Interlocking blocks need no mortar and self-align with lips or pins. Modular blocks follow standard 2:1 length-to-height ratios for stable pattern coursing.
📏
Height Limit Guidance
Without engineering, keep walls under 4 feet. Taller walls need geogrid reinforcement, deeper burial, and a licensed engineer's stamped design for safety.
💧
Drainage Necessity
Always add gravel backfill and a drain pipe behind the wall. Hydrostatic pressure from trapped water is the #1 cause of retaining wall failure.

Calculation Formula

Blocks per row = ceil(Wall length × 12 / Block length) Rows = ceil(Wall height × 12 / Block height) Blocks = Blocks per row × Rows Cap blocks = Blocks per row Estimated cost = ceil(Blocks × $3)

Frequently Asked Questions

How many blocks do I need for a retaining wall?
Divide the wall length in inches by the block length to get blocks per row, then divide wall height in inches by block height to get the number of rows. Multiply blocks per row by rows for total blocks.
How tall can a landscaping block wall be?
Without engineering, most landscape block walls should stay under 4 feet. Walls over 4 feet typically require a permit, geogrid reinforcement, and a professional engineer's design.
